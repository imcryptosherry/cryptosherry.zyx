# The Future of Generative AI: How W3AI Fits In

The smooth user experience and simplicity of Generative AI have led to its rapid growth and massive adoption among everyday users over the last two years.

In this article, we discuss the key role AIOZ W3AI will play in the future development of Generative AI, whose market size is projected to surpass $350 billion by 2030.

**Let's dive in:**

Generative AI is a modern branch of artificial intelligence where deep-learning models are capable of generating high-quality text, audio, images, videos, etc, usually in response to users' prompts.

The launch of ChatGPT in November 2022 ushered in the current wave of Generative AI solutions developed over the last 22 months, skyrocketing the demand for GPUs that Generative AI Models can use to execute tasks effectively.

Last week, Nvidia - the leading GPU manufacturer - posted positive [financial results](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-announces-financial-results-for-second-quarter-fiscal-2025) in line with analysts' expectations, and its CEO - Jensen Huang - mentioned that they were "seeing the momentum of Generative AI accelerate.”

This statement is a testament to the massive unlocked potential of the Generative AI industry, which has continued to grow in leaps and bounds.

Before the current Generative AI boom, most of the computing resources allocated for AI development were used to train AI models to make accurate predictions when fed with new data (**Predictive AI**).

Compared to Predictive AI, Generative AI requires additional computational power (for executing inference tasks) and storage space (for storing newly generated data).

These additional computational requirements are an obstacle to many AI developers and businesses who want to develop and integrate Generative AI solutions, as they need to pay cloud service providers extra to utilize their infrastructure for computational tasks.

With Generative AI projected to produce 10% of all new data by 2025, the cost of Generative AI computation is set to rise further as competition for the limited computing resources of cloud service providers increases.

These growing costs pose a serious threat to the future growth of Generative AI since many developers and businesses might be priced out of the Generative AI development race soon - an outcome that can severely limit innovation and consolidate power in the hands of a few.
